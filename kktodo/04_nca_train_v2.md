# 04 — nca/train.py v2 (Multi-Step Rollout)

This explains what changed in train.py and why the first run collapsed.

---

## What you saw

NCA ran for about 1.5 seconds then collapsed to a solid color.
Pressing F jumped between different solid colors. Reset showed rectangles
moving briefly, then solid color again.

That's not a bad model. That's a model trained to do the wrong task.

---

## Why it collapsed: the distribution shift problem

Training v1 taught the NCA this task:
> "Given a real GS state, predict what one GS step produces."

But free run asks a completely different question:
> "Given your OWN previous output, keep going indefinitely."

These sound similar. They're not.

In training: every single input was a real GS state — clean, stable,
generated by GS physics. The NCA never had to deal with its own errors.

In free run: step 1 takes a real GS state. Step 2 takes the NCA's own output —
which is already slightly wrong. Step 3 takes an output of a slightly-wrong input.
The small errors compound every single step. By step 30 the input looks nothing
like what the NCA was trained on, and it collapses.

This is called "distribution shift" — the distribution of inputs at test time
(NCA outputs) is different from the distribution at training time (GS states).

---

## The fix: multi-step rollout training

Instead of: run NCA 1 step → compare to GS 1 step.

Do: for 8 steps in a row, run NCA on its own output and compare to GS
     at each step. Accumulate loss across all 8 steps.

The NCA's input at step 2 is its own output from step 1 — same as free run.
This forces the NCA to stay close to GS across a whole trajectory, not just
match one step from a clean starting point.

One extra fix: after each NCA step, the f and k channels (14 and 15) get
re-injected with the original values. The NCA's delta could drift these
accidentally. f and k are control inputs from outside, not state the NCA
should modify — so we lock them back in each step.

---

## What changed in train.py

Three additions:

**1. ROLLOUT_STEPS = 8** (new hyperparameter)
How many NCA steps to unroll in the loss computation.
8 is a good starting point — enough to expose instability, not so many
that gradients vanish before reaching step 1.

**2. make_loss_fn rewritten**
Old: run 1 NCA step, compute MSE vs 1 GS step.
New: loop ROLLOUT_STEPS times:
  - NCA step on its own output (not GS output)
  - GS step on GS output (ground truth trajectory)
  - Re-inject f/k into NCA grid
  - Accumulate MSE
Average the accumulated loss across all 8 steps.

Persistence loss is now applied to the final rollout state (step 8),
not step 1. This tests stability at a later point in the trajectory.

**3. Resume from checkpoint**
RESUME_FROM = 'nca/checkpoints/params_020000.pkl'
RESUME_STEP = 20000

Training picks up from global step 20000 and runs 30000 more steps,
saving as params_021000.pkl, params_022000.pkl, ... params_050000.pkl.

Optimizer state (Adam's momentum) is NOT saved/loaded — Adam will
re-warm its internal buffers over the first ~100 steps. You'll see
the loss jump around briefly at the start. That's normal.

---

## What to expect from the new training run

Pool init: same ~15 min as last time.

Step speed: SLOWER than v1. Each step now runs 8 NCA forward passes
instead of 1, plus 8 GS steps. Expect ~2-3x slower = maybe 2-2.5 hrs total.

Loss behavior:
- Starts higher than v1's final loss (rollout loss is inherently larger)
- Should fall steadily over the 30k steps
- Good target: pred_loss < 0.005 at step 50000

## Run it

    source /home/kk/ai-env/bin/activate
    cd /home/kk/projects/ml/somnivex
    python nca/train.py

Watch for the "Resuming from:" line in the startup printout to confirm
it found the checkpoint. Then watch pred_loss. It will initially be
higher than where v1 finished — that's expected, rollout loss measures
more than 1-step loss. Watch it fall.

---

## After training: test with run_free.py

run_free.py does NOT need any changes. It loads whatever checkpoint
you point it at. After training finishes, it defaults to params_020000.pkl.
Change the CHECKPOINT line to params_050000.pkl and run it again.

If patterns stay alive for more than 30 seconds — the rollout training worked.
If they stay alive for minutes and respond to F (regime changes) — it really worked.
